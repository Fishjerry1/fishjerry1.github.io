January 18, 2024

ICHSSA 3 PROJECT: NATIONAL OVC MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEM (NOMIS) 3.0 ROLLOUT IN KANO
Transforming health with digital solutions

On January 18, 2024, I was directly involved in the successful rollout of the National Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC) Management Information System (NOMIS) 3.0 in Kano State, a critical component of the USAID-funded Integrated Child Health and Social Services Award (ICHSSA 3) Project. This initiative was a significant step in enhancing Nigeria's national response to the HIV/AIDS epidemic by fundamentally improving data management for OVC programs across the state.

My responsibilities included overseeing aspects of the system's deployment and ensuring its readiness for field use. NOMIS 3.0, adopted by the Government of Nigeria as the national OVC database, is a robust web application designed to operate seamlessly in both offline and online modes, which is crucial for data collection in diverse and often remote field settings. I focused on verifying that its modular design and stringent data validation rules were effectively minimizing entry errors and upholding data quality, which is paramount for generating reliable reports and informing interventions.

The system's capability to generate essential reports, including donor-specific requirements and custom service statistics, was a key area I monitored, as it provides timely insights into client lists and OVC status updates. Furthermore, I ensured that the flexible data transmission features—through export, import, and synchronization with a central server—were functioning optimally, making field data readily accessible for analysis and project management.

This deployment, which I helped to facilitate, is a testament to our commitment to leveraging electronic records for improved program outcomes and data-driven decision-making in the fight against HIV/AIDS and its impact on vulnerable children.

September 10 - 16, 2024

TRAINING OF SACA M&E OFFICERS IN BAUCHI ON THE ICHSSA 3 PROJECT
Advanced DHIS2, QGIS & Power BI for HIV Monitoring

From September 10 to 16, 2024, I played a key role in a crucial capacity-building initiative in Bauchi, where I contributed to the comprehensive training of State AIDS Control Agency (SACA) Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E) Officers. This training was an integral component of the USAID-funded Integrated Child Health and Social Services Award (ICHSSA 3) Project, which is dedicated to reducing the impact of HIV/AIDS on orphans and vulnerable children in Nigeria.

My involvement focused on delivering practical sessions and guiding officers through the intricacies of M&E. I addressed the critical need for enhanced M&E capacity at the sub-national level, recognizing that while M&E systems are designed to generate data for evidence-based decision-making, data utilization can often be limited among stakeholders, particularly at state and local government levels. The curriculum I helped to deliver covered fundamental M&E principles and practices, equipping officers with the essential skills to effectively track program activities, outputs, and outcomes.

Key areas included developing robust M&E strategies and frameworks, understanding indicator measures, and utilizing appropriate tools for data collection and analysis. By strengthening the M&E capabilities of SACA officers in Bauchi, I directly contributed to the ICHSSA 3 project's overarching goal of ensuring quality service provision and improving the socioeconomic conditions of OVC and their households.

This investment in local human resources is vital for institutionalizing a national data flow system, enabling seamless data transmission from communities and health facilities up to federal levels, and ultimately fostering a more coordinated and effective HIV/AIDS response in Nigeria.

January 15, 2025

Supervisor – USAID National Data Quality Assessment (DQA), ICHSSA 3 Project
Field Coordination Across 5 LGAs in Jigawa States & Protocol Development

On January 15, 2025, I proudly served as a Supervisor for the USAID National Data Quality Assessment (DQA) conducted under the Integrated Child Health and Social Services Award (ICHSSA 3) Project. This was a critical leadership role where I oversaw the meticulous process of verifying the accuracy, completeness, and reliability of data collected for this vital HIV/AIDS and OVC program.

Data quality assurance is the cornerstone of effective Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E), directly impacting program effectiveness, decision-making, and accountability. My responsibilities included leading a systematic review of data collection processes and conducting rigorous data quality checks. This involved selecting random samples of data points and meticulously comparing them against source documents and original data collection forms to identify and rectify any inconsistencies or errors.

A significant aspect of my role was proactively addressing common challenges such as data collection errors. I ensured that our team adhered to established data collection protocols, provided comprehensive training for data collectors, and implemented continuous supervision and spot-checks during the data collection phase to promptly identify and rectify errors. By ensuring the integrity of the data, this DQA, under my supervision, provided reliable evidence for USAID and implementing partners to make informed decisions, track program performance accurately, and ultimately enhance the impact of the ICHSSA 3 Project in supporting vulnerable populations.

September 2024

ICHSSA 3 PROJECT: TECHNICAL SUPPORT TO YOBE STATE
Supportive Supervision Visit

In September 2024, I provided comprehensive technical support to Yobe State as part of the Integrated Child Health and Social Services Award (ICHSSA 3) Project. This targeted intervention was designed to bolster the state's capacity in delivering high-quality, integrated health and social services to orphans and vulnerable children (OVC) affected by HIV/AIDS.

My technical support encompassed a range of critical areas, tailored to the specific needs identified within Yobe State's health and social welfare systems. This included strengthening local health information systems, enhancing data management practices, and improving the overall quality of service delivery for OVCs. Given the project's focus on HIV/AIDS, my work was instrumental in optimizing patient management and monitoring, leveraging electronic medical record systems like NOMIS (National OVCs Management Information System) for efficient data capture and analysis related to HIV care and treatment.

Furthermore, the technical assistance I provided aimed to reinforce the capacity of CSO and CCMWs to provide sustainable services, aligning with the broader goal of advancing self-reliance in health outcomes. Through my direct engagement, I ensured that Yobe State was equipped with the necessary tools, knowledge, and systems to effectively implement the ICHSSA 3 project's interventions, ultimately contributing to improved well-being and resilience for vulnerable children and their households.
